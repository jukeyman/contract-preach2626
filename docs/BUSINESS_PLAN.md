## 2. Market Opportunity
The U.S. Federal Government is the world's largest buyer of goods and services, spending over $700 billion annually. Under federal mandate, agencies must allocate:
* **23%** of prime contracts to small businesses.
* **5%** to Small Disadvantaged Businesses (including SBA 8(a)).
* **5%** to Women-Owned Small Businesses (WOSB).
* **3%** to Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Businesses (SDVOSB).
* **3%** to HUBZone-certified businesses.

Despite these mandates, most small businesses fail due to the complexity of SAM.gov registrations, socio-economic certifications, and highly technical request for proposal (RFP) cycles.
